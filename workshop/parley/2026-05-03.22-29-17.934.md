Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas), deforestation, and industrial processes. Here's a concise overview:

## Key Points

**Cause:** The enhanced greenhouse effect. Burning fossil fuels releases CO₂ and other greenhouse gases that trap heat in the atmosphere, preventing it from escaping into space.

**Major Greenhouse Gases:**
- Carbon dioxide (CO₂) — fossil fuels, deforestation
- Methane (CH₄) — agriculture, landfills, natural gas
- Nitrous oxide (N₂O) — fertilizers, combustion
- Fluorinated gases — industrial processes

**Observed Effects:**
- ~1.1°C rise in global average temperature since pre-industrial times
- Shrinking ice sheets and glaciers
- Rising sea levels
- More frequent and intense extreme weather events (heatwaves, droughts, hurricanes)
- Ocean acidification
- Shifts in ecosystems and biodiversity loss

**Key Mitigation Strategies:**
- Transitioning to renewable energy (solar, wind, nuclear)
- Improving energy efficiency
- Electrifying transportation
- Carbon capture and reforestation
- International agreements (e.g., the Paris Agreement aims to limit warming to 1.5–2°C)
